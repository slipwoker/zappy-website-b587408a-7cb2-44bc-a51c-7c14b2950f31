How Do Bail Bonds Work?

A bail bond is a financial guarantee to the court that the defendant will appear for all scheduled hearings. When you can't afford the full bail amount, a bail bondsman posts a surety bond for a non-refundable premium, typically 10% of the total bail. This allows the defendant to be released from custody while awaiting trial.

What Information Do I Need to Provide?

To get started, we'll need the defendant's full legal name, date of birth, the name and location of the jail where they're being held, the booking number if available, and the bail amount set by the judge. Having this information ready helps us move faster.
